Galagos Country Estate

The wattle forest surrounding Galagos, a native Australian plant naturalised in South Africa, provides an ideal habitat for the native galago population. Having exceptional night vision and agility the galago can catch prey whilst leaping from tree to tree. Feeding primarily on insects such as moths and grasshoppers and the occasional tasty treat of acacia gum, a family of tree to which the wattle belongs.

The most common galago spotted at Galagos Country Estate is the Southern Lesser Bushbaby (Galago moholi). Growing to only 37cm, which includes the bushy tail, and weighing only 150g the Moholi bushbaby is one of the smallest primates of the African continent. They form part of a group of 25 cryptic species of bushbaby, species closely related to each other but not yet classified.
